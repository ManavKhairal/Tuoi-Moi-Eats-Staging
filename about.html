The Trip That Changed Everything

In 2022, three friends packed their bags and boarded a flight to Vietnam. They weren't chefs. They weren't restaurateurs. They were just three curious souls who loved food and wanted to explore the streets of Hanoi.

What they didn't know was that this trip would change their lives forever.

✈️
Hanoi, Vietnam
The beginning of a dream

"We looked at each other and knew — this was the food we wanted to bring home."

— The Founders

The First Bite
The Revelation

One Bite.
Silence.

It was a humid evening in Hanoi's Old Quarter. The streets were alive with the sizzle of grills, the clatter of chopsticks, and the laughter of locals gathered around tiny plastic stools.

They stopped at a modest street-side stall. The owner, an elderly Vietnamese woman with a warm smile, handed them a plate of Bánh Xèo — a crispy, golden crepe filled with shrimp and fresh herbs.

The crunch, the burst of flavours, the balance of sweet, savoury, and sour — it was unlike anything they had ever tasted in Delhi.

"One bite. Silence. The crunch, the burst of flavours... it was unlike anything they had ever tasted."

The Realization

"In Delhi, we have everything — but we were missing this. The soul of Vietnamese food isn't just about taste. It's about balance. It's about freshness. It's about sharing a meal with strangers who become friends."

That's when the idea sparked: What if we could recreate this in Delhi?

The Birth

Tuổi Mới Eats

On a warm evening in Humayunpur, they opened the doors to Tuổi Mới Eats — a small, humble space with a big dream.

The name? "Tuổi Mới" loosely translates to "New Generation" — a nod to their fresh take on traditional Vietnamese cuisine while honouring its roots.

Every dish on the menu tells a story. The Phở made with broth that simmers for over 12 hours. The Bánh Mì served on fresh baguettes with pickled vegetables. The Gỏi Cuốn — summer rolls that are light, fresh, and bursting with herbs.
